Did users really leave Coupang? We reviewed spend, fresh installs, and churn across six rival apps

S
Sanga Lee · Staff Writer
8 Mar 2026 · UK tone sample
#K-Tech#Coupang#ConsumerShift#MarketSignals

“Do people in Korea genuinely have a practical alternative to Coupang?”

That question has kept returning in Korean online communities since last year’s privacy controversy.

At first glance, the narrative looked straightforward: public frustration, then mass exit. In practice, consumer behaviour has been rather more nuanced.

🤔

In Korea, Coupang is not merely an ecommerce app. For many households, it functions as everyday infrastructure—rapid delivery, breadth of stock, and membership convenience in one place.

So you often see a familiar split: people say they are “done” with a platform, yet continue to rely on it when speed or reliability matters most.

To test whether sentiment translated into action, we looked at behavioural indicators: payment flows, new installs, and churn signals across six competing services.

So, what actually changed?

Coupang’s core position remained robust—but movement at the margins became visible.

Rather than a single dramatic switch, we found selective migration: users adopted secondary apps for particular categories while keeping Coupang for urgency-led purchases.

In other words, this was less an exodus and more a rebalancing of shopping habits.

In the full version, we break down where this pressure is accumulating, which categories are most exposed, and where rivals are quietly gaining traction.
