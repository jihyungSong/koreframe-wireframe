Did users really leave Coupang? We checked spending, new installs, and churn across 6 rival apps

이
Sanga Lee · Staff Writer

#K-Tech#Coupang#ConsumerShift#MarketSignals

“Do people really have an alternative to Coupang?”

That question kept resurfacing in Korean communities after last year’s privacy controversy.

On the surface, it looked like a clean boycott narrative. In reality, user behavior moved in a much messier, more human way.

🤔

Because Coupang isn’t just another shopping app in Korea. For many users, it functions like infrastructure—delivery speed, product breadth, and membership perks bundled into daily life.

That creates a familiar gap: people may say “I’m leaving,” while still keeping the app for specific moments when convenience wins.

So instead of reading sentiment alone, we checked behavior data: spending trends, new installs, and churn signals across six competing platforms.

Here’s what stood out.

Coupang’s core dominance remained strong—but the edges started to shift.

Rather than a full “exit,” we saw selective migration: users adding backup apps for certain categories, while still relying on Coupang for speed-critical purchases.

In other words, this wasn’t a clean break. It was portfolio behavior.

From here, we unpack what that means for retention pressure, category-level vulnerability, and where competitors are quietly gaining ground.
